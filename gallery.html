Hybrid Images

Hybrid images change appearance depending on the distance (or size) at which they are viewed. Here are some examples synthesized using our method, where we condition low frequencies on one prompt and high frequencies on another.

Note these videos may not be optimized for your device or screen size. Try zooming in, standing a few feet away from the screen, or squinting if you can't see the image.

Triple Hybrid Images

By decomposing an image with a Laplacian pyramid, and conditioning each level of the pyramid with a different prompt, we can also produce hybrid images with three different contents.

Chromatic Steganography

By decomposing an image into a greyscale component and a color component, we can create images that change appearance when colorized.

Motion Blurring

We can also create images that change appearance under motion blurring.
